01 — Concept

Three glyphs tell the whole story.

logo system

The last three letters of opencoo — c · o · o — become the product's three states: open → compile → operate. Read across, that's the architecture of the product, drawn in ink.

c — open

Ingest. The open ring — open source, open to every source. Drive, Notion, Asana, Fireflies. Anything that produces text.

o — compile

Synthesize. The filled mark — a compiled wiki page. Not a chunk, not an embedding. A finished paragraph that an agent can actually read.

o — operate

Act. The ringed mark — an agent orbit. Heartbeat, Lint, Chat, Surfacer, Builder. Reading the middle glyph, writing back to the world.

02.5 — Motion

The wordmark compiles itself.

intro · 3.2s · loop
motion/intro.html · live
BEATS · absolute time
0.0 – 1.2s write-in. opencoo draws on, letter by letter, left to right. Every glyph is a proper Bauhaus letter — including the c, which is drawn with its aperture on the right. 1.2 – 2.0s c opens. The c rotates 180° around its own center. The aperture flips to the left — the canonical ingest glyph. The letter becomes the state. 1.5 – 2.3s o compiles. Ink floods from the middle o's center outward, filling the ring, leaving the small inner hole — the compile glyph. 2.0 – 2.5s o operates. A dot fades in at the center of the last o — the agent takes up orbit. 3.2s – ∞ heartbeat. Only the outer ring of the operate glyph breathes (scale 1 → 1.055 → 1, 1.6s cycle). The dot stays fixed — the agent's identity doesn't flicker; its orbit does.
PRINCIPLES
  • Never animate the wordmark arbitrarily. It either writes in or it transforms into state.
  • Pen-entry points: top (12 o'clock) for every ring; top for stems; left for bars.
  • Eases: cubic-bezier(.55,.1,.2,1) for writing, cubic-bezier(.6,.05,.2,1) for transformations.
  • Heartbeat is the only loop — nothing else cycles.
open full screen ↗ use as: landing hero · clip opener · social intro

Intro is autoplay + loop every 8s; the heartbeat fills the gap so the mark never sits dead on screen.

03 — Color

Paper, ink, one advisor's amber.

oklch · p3-safe

A warm-neutral paper system carries the product — it should read as printed documentation, not a SaaS landing page. One accent, Advisory Amber, is reserved for the agent layer — heartbeat reports, approvals, the voice of the COO. Wiki Teal marks compiled knowledge — citations, wiki chrome.

DO

Lead with paper + ink. Use Advisory exclusively for agent-generated surfaces — Heartbeat cards, approval prompts, the left rail. Use Wiki for citations and `path/to/page.md` chrome. Keep accent coverage under 10% per screen.

DON'T

No gradients on the mark. No purple. No blue-gray "enterprise SaaS" palette. Advisory amber is never used for destructive actions (that's Alert). Never pair Advisory next to Alert without a paper buffer between them.

Never fully rounded. opencoo surfaces are documents, not pills. Cards inherit the 6 px radius; pill-shaped buttons and fully-rounded cards are forbidden.

09 — Voice

How opencoo writes about itself.

copy · tone
DO · SAY IT LIKE THIS

"Compiled at ingestion, not retrieved at query time."
"Agents navigate by path."
"The wiki is the product."
"One process by default. Split when you need to."

Short sentences. Concrete nouns. Technical specificity over marketing. Lowercase product name, always.

DON'T · NEVER THIS

"Revolutionary AI-powered enterprise knowledge platform."
"Unlock insights from your data with semantic search."
"OpenCoo · OpenCOO · Open Coo."
Emoji. Gradients. 🚀 🎉 💡 — never.
